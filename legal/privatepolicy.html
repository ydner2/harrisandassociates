Privacy Policy

We collect Non-Public Personal Information about You from the Following Sources:

  • Information you provide to us on applications and other forms
  • Your personal business dealings with us and insurance companies
  • Information that we receive from the Department of Motor Vehicles or other Consumer agencies
  • Information that is contained in medical records or from medical professionals that is related to insurance claims

Unless it is specifically stated otherwise in an amended privacy notice, no other information will be collected about you.

Categories of information that may be disclosed:

  • Information about your transactions with us, our affiliates or other, such as your policy coverage, premiums, and payment history; and
  • Information from your application or other forms, such as your name, address, social security number, assets, income and beneficiaries
  • Information from consumer reporting agencies, such as your credit history

To whom we may disclose information:

We do not disclose any non-public personal information about our clients or former clients to anyone except as permitted by law. We may disclose non-public personal information about you to the following:

  • Governmental agencies when required to do so
  • Insurance institution, agent, or credit reporting agency
  • Group policy holders
  • Lienholders, Mortgagee, Loss Payee, Certificate Holders, Assignee, or any other person or organization having a legal or beneficial interest in the policy of insurance
  • To persons or agencies lawfully entitled pursuant to a subpoena or court order
  • Medical care institutions or medical care professionals when required

Your Right to Opt Out

If you do not want us to disclose personal information about you to any third parties, you may tell us now by "Opting Out". By "Opting Out" you will instruct us not to share or disclose any non-public personal information with insurance companies or any third parties for the purpose of obtaining insurance services. If you wish to "Opt Out", call us. We may share information about you if we do not hear from you within thirty (30) days. However, you may "Opt Out" at any time by contacting us.

If you do "Opt Out", Harris & Associates Insurance Agency may still disclose information as required by law.

We continue to protect your private information regardless of your Opt Out decision.

Your right also includes the right to request corrections, amendments or deletion of any information that is in our possession. You may notify us in writing if you believe any information should be corrected, amended, or deleted and we will review your request. We will either make the requested change or explain to you why we did not honor your request. You have the right to challenge any disputed information, which will be included in all future disclosures of your information.
